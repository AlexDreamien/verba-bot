# Verba — Administrator Guide

There are two kinds of "admin":

1. **Group admin** — runs the competition in their chat (seasons, language). Being
   a Telegram administrator/owner of the group is enough.
2. **Bot operator** — the owner of the installation (their Telegram ID is in
   `ADMIN_IDS`). They get the broadcast and server settings.

Bot: **@VerbaGame_bot**. Competition commands only work in groups.

---

## Part 1. Group admin

### Adding the bot to a group
1. Add **@VerbaGame_bot** to the group chat.
2. (Recommended) In **@BotFather → /setprivacy → pick the bot → Disable**. A normal
   bot can't see the member list; with **privacy disabled** it sees all messages
   and tracks who plays more accurately. With privacy on it only sees commands and
   mentions — workable, but narrower coverage.
3. Tell members: to join the competition send **`/register`** in the group (or tap
   the **Play** button — it both registers them and opens the game).

> The game can't be opened by a button directly in a group (a Telegram
> limitation), so the **Play** button leads to the bot's direct chat. That's normal.

> **Group anti-spam.** The bot replies to players in their DM for everything
> except registration, the leaderboard and win announcements. In the `/` hint,
> regular players in a group see only `register/unregister/play/help`; admins also
> see `stats/seasons/startseason/finishseason`. A non-admin can pull the
> leaderboard once per hour; admins anytime.

### Group language
**`/lang`** in the group sets the language the bot uses in that chat (tables,
announcements). It doesn't affect the game's languages — players choose those in
the game itself.

### Seasons
The competition runs in **seasons**. Every group starts in **season 1**
automatically — there's no need to "start" the first season.

| Command | Who | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| `/startseason` | group admin | start a **new** season (the visible table resets) |
| `/finishseason` | group admin | finish the season and post the **standings** |
| `/seasons` | anyone | list of past-season champions |
| `/stats` | anyone | current table + season number |

**Rolling a season over:**
1. `/finishseason` — the bot posts the final table and **remembers the champion**
   (1st place) in history.
2. `/startseason` — the next season begins, the table resets (history of past
   seasons is preserved and available via `/seasons`).

Notes:
- While a season is **finished and the next hasn't started**, games still count
  toward personal stats but **earn no competition points** (the competition is
  paused).
- `/startseason` can't be used while a season is running — `/finishseason` first.
- Only group admins (or the bot operator from `ADMIN_IDS`) can manage seasons.

### How the competition works (to answer players)
- A **round** = one word, one language, one day. 3 rounds a day (ru/uk/en); points
  per language are independent.
- **Win points:** `7 − number of tries` (1 try = 6 … 6 tries = 1).
- **+3 bonus** for whoever is **first in the group** to solve that round.
- A **win announcement** goes to the chat only for the **first** solver of a round,
  **without the word**, at most 3 times a day (once per language).
- A **skip (💤)** is only counted for languages a player already played that season
  (so single-language players aren't punished).
- Table tie-break is by lower average number of tries (⌀).

## Part 2. Bot operator

### Broadcast
| Command | Who | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| `/broadcast` | `ADMIN_IDS` only | immediately send today's game to all subscribers |

Besides the manual broadcast there's a **schedule**:
- a daily auto-broadcast (if `BROADCAST_CRON` is set);
- **day close** at `CLOSE_DAY_AT`: unfinished games are marked, skips are recorded
  in active seasons, and a day summary is sent to the operator (and optionally to
  subscribers).

### Environment variables (`.env`)
Core: `BOT_TOKEN`, `ADMIN_IDS` (comma-separated), `WEBAPP_URL` (HTTPS Mini App
address), `DB_PATH`, `WEB_HOST`/`WEB_PORT`, `TZ` (default Europe/Kyiv),
`BROADCAST_CRON`, `CLOSE_DAY_AT`, `INIT_DATA_MAX_AGE`. Full list and an example are
in `.env.example` and `README.md`.

### The "/" command menu
The command hints in Telegram are registered automatically at startup
(`setMyCommands`, localized ru/uk/en). New commands may appear in the client with
a delay — re-entering the chat helps.

### Sharing results into chats (optional)
By default the in-game "Share" button **copies** the result grid to the clipboard.
To let players send it **inline into any chat**:
1. **@BotFather → /setinline** — enable inline mode for the bot (you can set a
   placeholder).
2. In `webapp/config.js` set `window.VERBA_INLINE_SHARE = true` and deploy the
   webapp.

The bot already answers the inline query (echoes the grid + a link to the game).

### Dictionaries and the word of the day
- The answer pools (~365 words per language) and the dictionary of accepted words
  are generated by `tools/build_words.py` into `webapp/words.js`.
- The word of the day is picked **deterministically** from the date and a
  **shuffled** pool (client-side, offline). The server doesn't store the word —
  competition integrity is trust-based (like the original Wordle). Full anti-cheat
  would require server-side guess validation and was intentionally not done.

### Deploy
- The **Mini App (webapp/)** is published to **GitHub Pages** automatically on push
  to `main` (`.github/workflows/pages.yml`).
- The **backend** (bot + result collector) runs on Fly.io: `flyctl deploy`.
- Liveness: `https://<app>.fly.dev/healthz` → `{"ok": true}`.
- Details are in `README.md` and `CLAUDE.md`.

## FAQ
- **A player isn't in the table.** They didn't `/register` in this group (or left
  via `/unregister`).
- **A win wasn't announced.** Only the **first** solver of a round is announced;
  others score silently. Or the season isn't active.
- **Someone has lots of 💤.** Skips only count for "their" languages — it means
  they started playing those languages but didn't finish on some days.
- **Today's word "changed" after an update.** A one-off cosmetic effect on the day
  the pools/shuffle are updated; from the next day everything is normal.
